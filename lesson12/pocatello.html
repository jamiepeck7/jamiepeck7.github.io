Pocatello Happenings

History of Pocatello

Gateway to the Northwest

Founded in 1889, Pocatello was known as the "Gateway to the Northwest." As pioneers, gold miners and settlers traveled the Oregon Trail, they passed through the Portneuf Gap south of town. Stage and freight lines and the railroad soon followed, turning our community into a trade center and transportation junction.

Indigenous Tribes

The name "Pocatello" comes from an chief from the Native American Shoshone tribe. He granted the railroad passage through the Fort Hall Indian Reservation.
Bannock and Shoshone tribes occupied southeasternn Idaho for many years before the trek by Lewis and Clark across Idaho. Many fur trappers and traders were drawn to the area after hearing the reports of the resources avaiable here.

Agriculture

With the Snake River nearby, the region is a large supplier of potatoes, grains and other crops.

Idaho State University

Pocatello is home to Idaho State Unversity. It serves more than 12,000 students from around the world. ISU is a Carnegie-classified doctoral research and teaching institution with more than 200 programs spanning from energy, health professions, nuclear research, teaching, humanities, engineering, performing and visual arts, technology, biological sciences, pharmacy, business and so much more added each year. ISU is the home of the Bengals and boasts many sucessful sports programs and teams.
